Restricted Groups allows the administrator to configure local groups on client computers. For example, you could add a helpdesk support group to all clients on your desktop. To learn how to configure local groups on your client computer using Group Policy rather than visiting each computer to make the changes, proceed with the following steps...
  • Begin first by selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then select 'Group Policy Management'...
  • Once in 'Group Policy Management', we'll expand the OU 'New York'.
  • Inside our 'New York' OU you'll notice that there is a Group Policy named 'New York Office'. For this example, we are going to configure this Group Policy to create a new a group to the local 'Administrators' group of every computer that lies uder this 'New York' OU. To do this, we'll first right-click on the 'New York Office' GPO...
  • And select 'Edit...'.
  • Once the Group Policy is open for editing, start by first expaning 'Policies' under 'Computer Configuration'.
  • Then expand 'Windows Settings'.
  • Next, expand 'Security Settings'.
  • And then select 'Restricted Groups'.
  • After selecting 'Restricted Groups', right-click it...
  • And select 'Add Group...'.
  • As stated earlier, we're going to add a group to the local 'Administrators' group of every computer that lies uder this 'New York' OU. The approach we're going to take in this example is essentially remove every member from the local 'Administrators' group and readd back the ones we want. To do this, first type into the text field 'Administrators'.

  • Then press 'OK'.
  • To reset group membership, we'll want to use the top section of this form, 'Members of this group:'.
  • To begin, click on the 'Add..' button.
  • Then press 'Browse...'.
  • Then enter in the group name you want to add. In this case, we will start with the 'domain admins' group. This group is added automatically to the local administrators group when the computer is added to the domain. It is important NOT to forget to add this group back, otherwise your domain administrators will have their administrator access revoked.

  • Press 'OK' when finished.
  • Press 'OK' to add the group.
  • Now we will add our new group to the local 'Administrator' group. To do this, again press the 'Add...' button.
  • Then 'Browse...'.
  • The new group we are going to add is called 'helpdesk administrators'. This is a group we created for the helpdesk technicians. This will give them administrator access to the client operating systems without having to give them domain administrator access.

  • Then press 'OK'.
  • Press 'OK' to add the group.
  • So now, as you can see, we have two groups that are members of the local 'Administrators' group. This was done by essentially removing everything that once existed in the local 'Administrators' group and then adding back the members we wanted. This option is useful for when an adimistrator wants to clear unwated users and groups from a group and only have the users and groups that is wanted.

  • However, 'Restricted Groups' also gives you the option to add users and groups to a group without having to first remove all its contents first and then rebuild, but to just simply add the users and groups that is wanted. To do this, we'll first close this form by clicking 'OK'.
  • And then close the GPO editor.
  • Back in 'Group Policy Management', we'll expand the 'Computers' OU found under the 'New York' OU.
  • Then, inside the 'Computers' OU, we have an OU called 'Training Lab'. If we exapand that OU...
  • You'll find a Group Policy called 'Training Lab Group Policy'. Right-click that GPO...
  • And select 'Edit...'.
  • Once the Group Policy is open for editing, we'll once again expand 'Policies' under 'Computer Configuration'.
  • Then expand 'Windows Settings'.
  • Next, expand 'Security Settings'.
  • And then select 'Restricted Groups'.
  • After selecting 'Restricted Groups', right-click it...
  • And select 'Add Group...'.
  • From here, press the 'Browse' button.
  • In this case, we want to add a group to the local 'Administrators' group, not reset the group and start from scratch. So, this time, instead of entering in the 'Administrators' group, we'll enter the group we want to add, which will be 'training lab administrators'.

  • Press 'OK' when finished.
  • Press 'OK'.
  • To add to group membership, we'll want to use the bottom section of this form, 'This group is a member of:'.
  • To begin, click on the 'Add..' button.
  • Then press 'Browse...'.
  • In here, we want to enter in the local 'Administrators' group.

  • Then press 'OK'.
  • And then press 'OK'.
  • Now what we have is the 'Training Lab Administrators' group will be a member of the local 'Administrators' group, and no resetting was involved, we just simply added the group as a member.

  • Press 'OK'.
