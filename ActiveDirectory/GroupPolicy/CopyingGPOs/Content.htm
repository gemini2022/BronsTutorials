To learn how to copy GPOs, proceed with the following steps...
  • Begin first by selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then select 'Group Policy Management'...
  • Once in 'Group Policy Management', Expand 'Group Policy Objects'.
  • And right-click the Group Policy Object you want to copy. In this case, we're going to right-click the 'New York Office' Group Policy Object.
  • And select 'Copy'.
  • The next step is to right-click 'Group Policy Objects'...
  • And select 'Paste'.
  • After selecting 'Paste', you will get the option to keep the existing permissions, or use the default permissions. If you are not sure, select the option 'Use the default permissions for new GPOs'.

  • Then press 'OK'.
  • It does not take long to copy the Group Policy.

 
  • Once complete, press 'OK'.
  • Now, as you can see, a copy of the 'New York Office' GPO has been created.
  • We can now rename this GPO by right-clicking it...
  • And selecting 'Rename'.
  • Selecting 'Rename' will give us the ability to rename our GPO.
  • In this case, we will rename the GPO to 'Washington Office'.
  • The next step is to link the Group Policy to the required OU. In this case, we will right-click the 'Washington' OU...
  • And select 'Link an Existing GPO...'.
  • You will then have the option to choose the GPO you want to link. In this case, we will select the 'Washington Office' GPO.

  • Then press 'OK'.
  • If we now expand the 'Washington' OU...
  • You can see a link to our 'Washington Office' GPO has been created.
