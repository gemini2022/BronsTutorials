To learn how to configure audit policies for the domain, proceed with the following steps...
  • Begin first by selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then select 'Group Policy Management'...
  • Once in 'Group Policy Management', you'll need to edit a Group Policy. In this case, we're going to edit the 'Domain Wide Group Policy'. To do this, right-click the Group Policy.
  • And then select 'Edit...'.
  • Once in the 'Group Policy Management Editor', the auditing settings can be found in 'Policies' under 'Computer Configuration'...
  • Then 'Windows Settings'.
  • And then, 'Security Settings'.
  • Then finally, 'Local Policies'.
  • Once in 'Local Polices', select 'Audit Policy'.

 
  • On the right side of the window, you will see nine different audit settings that can be configured.
  • If we right-click on 'Audit account logon events'.
  • And select 'Properties'.
  • Once in the 'Audit account logon events Properties', we can select the option 'Define these policy settings'.
  • After selecting the 'Define these policy settings' option, we will then get the choice to audit 'Success' and/or 'Failure'.
  • When choosing which settings to audit and which not to audit, it is worth having a look at the 'Explain' tab.
  • The 'Explain' tab will give you a detailed description of what the setting does.
