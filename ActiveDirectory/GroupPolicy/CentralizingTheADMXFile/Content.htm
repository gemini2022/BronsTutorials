To take advantage of the benefits of .admx files, you must create a Central Store in the SYSVOL folder on a domain controller. The Central Store is a file location that is checked by the Group Policy tools. The Group Policy tools use any .admx files that are in the Central Store. The files that are in the Central Store are later replicated to all domain controllers in the domain. To learn how to create a Central Store, proceed with the following steps...
  • Begin first by selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then select 'Group Policy Management'...
  • Once in 'Group Policy Management', right-click a group policy. In this case, we will right-click on the group policy we created earlier, 'Domain Wide Group Policy'.
  • Then select 'Edit...'.
  • In the 'Group Policy Management Editor', expand the 'Policies' folder.
  • Then hover your cursor over the 'Administrative Templates' folder
  • Once you hover over the 'Administrative Templates' folder, a tooltip will appear that informs you that policy definitions are currently being retrieved from the local machine. To make management of the ADMX files easier, we will move them to a central store. To do this, we will do the following:
  • First, go back and select 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Computer'...

 
  • And open your local hard disk of your computer.
  • Then go into the 'Windows' folder.
  • Once in the 'Windows' folder, right-click on the 'PolicyDefinitions' folder.
  • And select 'Copy'.
  • While still in the 'Windows' folder, go the the 'SYSVOL' folder and open that.
  • Then open the 'domain' folder.
  • And then finally open the 'Policies' folder.
  • In the 'Policies' folder, there will be a folder for each of the group policies that have been created in the domain.
  • The next step is to create the central store. Paste the 'PolicyDefinitions' folder you coppied eariler to this location.
  • Now that you have the 'PolicyDefinitions' folder coppied inside the 'Policies' folder, the ADMX files will now be retrieved from a local store. To make sure that this is the case, we will do the following:
  • Once again, go back and select 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then select 'Group Policy Management'...
  • Once in 'Group Policy Management', right-click a group policy. Again, we will right-click on the group policy we created earlier, 'Domain Wide Group Policy'.
  • Then select 'Edit...'.
  • Once in the 'Group Policy Management Editor again', expand the 'Policies' folder.
  • Also, things relating to security settings can be configured here.
  • Now when you hover over the 'Administrative Templates' folder, a tooltip will appear that informs you that policy definitions are now being retrieved from the central store.
