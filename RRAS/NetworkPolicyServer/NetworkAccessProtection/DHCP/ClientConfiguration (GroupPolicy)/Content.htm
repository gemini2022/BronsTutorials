To learn how to configure NAP settings for a client using Group Policy, proceed with the following steps...
  • Begin first by selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then 'Group Policy Management'...
  • Once in Group Policy Management, expand 'Forest'.
  • Then expand 'Domains'.
  • Then expand a domain, which in this case is 'bronstutorials.local'.
  • Once a domain has been expanded, right-click on a Group Policy, which in this case will be 'Default Domain Policy'.
  • Then select 'Edit...'. This will open the Group Policy Management Editor.
  • Once in the Group Policy Management Editor, expand 'Computer Configuration'.
  • Then expand 'Policies'.
  • And then expand 'Windows Settings'.
  • And then finally expand 'Security Settings'.
  • Under 'Security Settings', select 'System Services'.
  • Once in System Services, scroll down untill you get to 'Network Access Protection Agent'.
  • Once at 'Network Protection Agent', double-click it to get to the properties of the service.
  • Once in the properties of the Network Access Protection Agent service, select the option 'Define this policy setting'.
  • Then select 'Automatic' so that this services automatically starts when the client computer boots up.
  • Then press 'OK'.
  • Next, go down and expand 'Network Access Protection'.
  • Then expand 'NAP Client Configuration'.
  • After you expand 'NAP Client Configuration', select 'Enforcement Clients'.
  • Then make your way to the right side of the window and double-click on 'DHCP Quarantine Enforcement Client'.
  • Then select the option 'Enable this enforcement client'. Enabling this setting allows NAP to be enforced on the client.
  • Once the setting is enabled, press 'OK'.
  • We next have to configure a setting under Administrative Templates, to do this, scroll down.
  • Then, once you get to 'Administrative Templates', expand it.
  • Then expand 'Windows Components'.
  • Once 'Windows Components' has been expanded, scroll down until you get to 'Security Center'.
  • Once you get to 'Security Center', select it.
  • And then to the right of the window, double-click on 'Turn on Security Center'.
  • From here, select the option 'Enabled'.
  • And then press 'OK'.
