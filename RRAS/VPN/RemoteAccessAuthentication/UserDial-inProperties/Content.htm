To learn how to allow remote access for a user, proceed with the following steps...
  • Begin first by selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then select 'Active Directory Users and Computers'...
  • Once in 'Active Directory Users and Computers', expand your domain, in this case it's 'bronstutorials.local'.
  • Once you expand your domain, click on the 'Users' OU.
  • Once you click on the 'Users' OU, look to right side of the window. There you will see a list of all the users in the 'Users' OU. For this example we will allow remote access for the Administrator. To do this, right-click on the user 'Administrator'.
  • And then select 'Properties'.
  • Once in the properties of the Administrator, select the 'Dial-in' tab.
  • At the top of the tab under 'Network Access Permission', select the option 'Allow access'.
  • The Administrator now has remote access. Selecting this option overrides any NPS policy that may have been configured for this user.

  • Press 'OK'.
