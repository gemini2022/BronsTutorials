Enterprise certification authorities use certificate templates to define the format and content of certificates, to specify which users and computers can enroll for which types of certificates, and to define the enrollment process, such as autoenrollment, enrollment only with authorized signatures, and manual enrollment. A preconfigured list of certificatest allows users and computers to enroll for certificates without having to create complex certificate requests. You utilize these templates by duplicating an existing preconfigured certificate template and using the existing template's properties as the default for the new template. To learn how to obtain this list of preconfigured certificate templates, proceed with the following steps...
  • There are a couple of ways in which you can obtain a list of preconfigured certificate templates. One way is through the MMC Console. To do this, begin by first selecting 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • Then type 'mmc' in the search field.
  • Now click on 'mmc'. This will open the mmc console.
  • Once the 'mmc console' is open, select 'File' located in the upper left corner of your screen.
  • Then select 'Add/Remove Snap-in...'. This will open the 'Add or Remove Snap-ins' form.
  • When the 'Add or Remove Snap-ins' form opens, select the 'Certificate Templates' snap-in.
  • Then click on the 'Add' button to add it to the 'Selected snap-ins' list.
  • Once the 'Certificate Templates' snap-in is added to the 'Selected snap-ins' list, click 'OK'.
  • Back at the 'mmc console' in the upper left corner of the mmc console window, select 'Certificate Templates'.
  • Then, to the right of the window, a number of preconfigured certificate templates will be displayed.
  • Another way of obtaining a list of preconfigured certificate templates is through the Certification Authority Snap-In. To do this, go back down to 'Start' at the bottom left corner of your screen.
  • But this time go to 'Administrative Tools'...
  • Then 'Certification Authority'...
  • Once in Certification Authority, expand the root node for the CA, which in this case is 'EnterpriseCA'.
  • Then right-click 'Certificate Templates'.
  • And select 'Manage'.
  • And as you can see, the same list of preconfigured certificate templates is displayed.
